Welcome! My name is Sally and this is a documentary of my travels this past year in the French Riviera. During my trip, I visited a few cities in Europe and I'll be highlighting Marseille, France here.
Let's go!

Marseille

A Cathédrale de la Major

This Roman Catholic cathefral is a national monument of France. It is also close to the Le Panier and is worth visiting as you pass through the streets of the old town.

B Vieux Port à Marseille

This natural harbor is at the end of one of the main streets in Marseille. Along the water, you can ride a ferris wheel for a fantastic view of the city.

C Four des Navettes

This centuries-old bakery is the oldest in Marseille. The oven, built at the end of the 18th century, is still in use today to make thier signature navettes.

D Vallon des Auffes

This little fishing port is constitued by over 50 fisher cabins and boats. It boasts magnificient views of the turquoise water on the French Riviera.

E Notre Dame de la Garde

This symbolic cathedral is the most visited site in Marseille. It was built on the highest natural point in the city and remains unobstructed.
